Why You Should Be Staging Your Home

Imagine you just walked into an open house. The lights are on, it looks bright and inviting. You begin to picture your couch where theirs is, and that coffee table...it looks perfect. Everything ties in together and you instantly fall in love with this home. You think to yourself, "I could definitely see myself sitting here with a cup of coffee in the morning.". It all has to do with how you stage your home. And doing so in the right way can help sell your home a lot faster. I'll share some tips with you today that can help make all the difference between someone seeing it, and someone seeing themselves in it.

Kitchen
Plain oak cabinetry can be updated with paint and removal of the upper cabinet doors. To complete the update, add white bead-board to the back of the cabinets and back splash. This gives a backdrop and contrast color to display dishware. Color unifies a space, so here I added pops of a strong color with an old toolbox, and antiques globes and maps.

Master Bedroom
Grey walls update this tan and black master bedroom. Here, a monochromatic color scheme, texture, and special lighting add ambiance. When lighting a master bedroom, I use multiple lamps with low wattage light bulbs to give a relaxing, calming atmosphere.

Wall Art and Lighting
Here’s an idea to decorate a large wall. I took a collection of postcards and displayed them on old shutters. The louvers were perfect for clipping on the clothespins that attached each postcard to the shutter. For the lighting, I ripped off the fabric of an old lampshade and attached it to a ceiling pendent light. An exposed Edison light bulb updated old lamp.

Industrial Kitchen
“Industrial” is all the buzz in decorating right now. It’s an intentional blending of old materials with a modern function. To give this kitchen an industrial look, I replaced the upper cabinets with open shelving made from weathered wood and plumbing fittings. A wooden chandelier, antique fans, and old clipboards complete the industrial look.
